ACTIVE SHOOTER INCIDENT

  • If you believe an incident is a hostile event/active shooter immediately notify a Supervisor
    Initial Questions:
  • Do you/did you see the suspect(s)?
  • Are you injured or with someone who is?
  • Are you safe and out of danger?

    Tips for call takers with involved/on scene callers:
  • Preface advice to callers with "do what you think is safest"
  • If the caller can safely GET OUT (i.e. a nearby exit or climb out a window) advise them to avoid hallways & keep hands visible
  • If the caller is not VERY close to an exit advise them to not run through hallways.
  • If not able to safely GET OUT instruct the caller to HIDE OUT & KEEP OUT

    HIDE OUT & KEEP OUT
  • Secure and barricade immediate area (classroom, bathroom, office, closet) by:
  • Locking doors
  • Block door using furniture
  • Jam the door shut (wedge a 3 ring binder under the door)
  • Close blinds/shades
  • Turn off lights
  • Silence all electronic devices
  • Shelter behind items for additional protection

    Specific questions for involved/on scene callers

    Callers with visual of the suspect:
  • # of suspects. Is the suspect(s) communicating with others (i.e. talking on a cell phone)
  • Location of suspects
  • Direction of travel
  • FULL description of suspect(s)
  • Any body armor, gas masks
  • Weapons or explosives
  • Has the suspect taken hostages
  • Was the suspect carrying anything
  • Did you hear the suspect say anything
  • Any Vehicle Information

    On scene callers (HIDE OUT & KEEP OUT)
  • If the caller is secured with an injured party and they have access to an outer window have them put a sign or hang something in the window so responders can identify where medical will be needed.
  • Obtain the number of casualties and an estimate of the number of people at their secured location. Office or classroom number - get a specific location within the location (i.e. first floor east side)
  • Updates of sound of additional gun shots (time frame/direction of sound).
  • Gather information regarding building layout; access points, elevators, stairwells
  • Advise the caller that the contact teams will not stop to help injured persons, until they sweep the building to locate the suspect(s).
  • Inform callers to follow officers instructions - keep your hands on your head, leave personal belongings behind.
