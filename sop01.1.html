Statement

 

The Montgomery County Department of Public Safety Emergency Communications Division is a Public Safety Answering Point for police, fire, emergency medical and emergency management services within the County. The County created its first Communications Center in 1956 and had an initial staff of 6 employees.

 

During normal, day-to-day operations or unusual situations, it is imperative to identify and maintain a chain of command within the Division, and establish written protocols that give authority to act and respond.  Division employees should be well informed of the command structure and all written policies.

 

Policy 

 

The safety and welfare of the public are responsibilities shared by all levels of government. The authority of those responsible for the operations of the Emergency Communications Division will be documented in the Division Standard Operating Procedures (SOP) Manual, and will be in accordance with local, Commonwealth, and Federal law.

  

The Public Safety Director’s authority originates within directives at all levels of government regulating emergency management functions.  In Title 35 of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania, Part V, Chapter 75, authority is given to organize and be responsible for emergency management, response and recovery within the territorial limits of Montgomery County.

 

Procedure

 

1.      Management

  

The Emergency Communications Division is a division of the Montgomery County Department of Public Safety.

  

1.1.            The Director of Public Safety is responsible for the overall operation of the Department of Public Safety.

 

1.2.            The Deputy Director of Public Safety for Emergency Communications is responsible for the overall operations of the Emergency Communications Division. The Deputy Director of Public Safety for 9-1-1 Program Support is responsible for the overall support functions of the Emergency Communications Division.

 

1.2.1.      Emergency Communications Operational staff:

                          

·         Assistant Director for Professional Development

·         Assistant Director for Fire & Rescue Coordination

·         Assistant Director for Special Operations

 

1.2.2.      Emergency Communications Support Staff:

 

·         Assistant Director for Professional Standards

·         Assistant Director for Technical Services

·         9-1-1 Systems Coordinator

·         Public Safety Intelligence Coordinator

·         Public Safety Technology Coordinator

 

2.      Chain of Command

 

2.1.            Operations personnel will use the following chain of command:

 

·         Director of Public Safety

·         Deputy Director for Emergency Communications

·         Platoon Commander

·         Shift Supervisor

·         Telecommunicator

 

2.2.            Trainees will use the following chain of command:

 

·         Director of Public Safety

·         Deputy Director for Emergency Communications

·         Assistant Director for Professional Development

·         Communications Training Officer (CTO)

·         Trainee

 

2.3.            Support personnel will use the following chain of command:

 

·         Director of Public Safety

·         Deputy Director for 9-1-1 Program Support

·         Assistant Director or Coordinator

 

3.      Line of Succession

 

3.1.            In the event that the Director of Public Safety is unable to command, and to ensure uninterrupted leadership, authority for the Division will follow a line of succession from the Deputy Director for Emergency Communications down to the Senior Telecommunicator:

 

·         Deputy Director for Emergency Communications

·         Deputy Director for 9-1-1 Program Support

·         Assistant Director for Professional Development

·         Assistant Director for Professional Standards

·         Assistant Director for Special Operations

·         Assistant Director for Fire & Rescue Coordination

·         Assistant Director for Technical Services

·         Platoon Commander

·         Senior Supervisor on duty

·         Senior Telecommunicator according to length of service

 

4.      Authority and Responsibility

 

4.1.            Throughout the Division all personnel are vested with authority to properly execute their duties and are responsible and accountable for the authority vested in them.

 

4.2.            All personnel are accountable for the use of delegated authority.

 

4.3.            Personnel in supervisory positions will be held accountable for the actions of employees under their immediate control.

 

5.      Issuance of Lawful Orders

 

5.1.            Following the line of succession authority, lawful orders may be given to Division employees to ensure the efficient operation of the Emergency Communications Division.

 

5.2.            All employees are required to promptly obey the lawful order(s) of a superior, or an employee fulfilling the responsibilities of a superior, during normal operations or unusual situations.

 

5.2.1.      Lawful orders from a superior, but being relayed by another employee are to be followed without hesitation.

 

5.2.2.      Disobedience or disregard of a lawful order is grounds for disciplinary action.

 

5.3.            If an employee receives a conflicting lawful order, the employee must advise the person giving the most recent order of the conflict. If the person giving the most recent order does not change the order, the employee will obey that order. The employee will not be held responsible for disregarding the older order.

 

5.4.            If an employee receives an order they believe may be unlawful, the employee will clarify the order with the person giving the order. The person issuing the order is responsible for ensuring the order is lawful. The employee will obey the questionable order unless they know the order is unlawful. If necessary, either employee may contact someone of higher authority.

 

5.5.            If an employee receives an unlawful order that they know violates local, Commonwealth, or Federal law, the employee will not obey the order. The employee will inform the person issuing the order that the order is unlawful. The person giving the order will clarify and/or revise the order so as to ensure its lawfulness. If necessary, either employee may contact someone of higher authority.

 

6.      Normal Operations

 

6.1.            During the typical, day-to-day operations, the Deputy Director for Emergency Communications is charged with authority for Division operations. Should an anticipated absence occur, an acting authority will be designated and notified.  

 

6.2.            The Deputy Director for Emergency Communications and/or 9-1-1 Program Support, under the delegated authority of the Director of Public Safety, may issue, modify, or approve the Division’s written policies and directives.

 

6.3.            The Division Assistant Directors and/or Coordinators are authorized to issue written directives and memorandums at the discretion of the Deputy Directors, and where associated with their Division discipline and responsibilities.

 

7.      Unusual Situations

 

7.1.            An unusual situation is a significant event or emergency, or a combination thereof, which creates a significant increase in workload or activity level within the Communications Center, or possesses an immediate threat to the life and/or safety of Division personnel. See SOP 7.1 Critical Incidents and Special Operations. The increase in the workload or activity level must exceed the normal course of activity during a busy day.  

 

7.2.            During unusual situations, a platoon supervisor, their designee, or an employee acting in a position of delegated authority has the ability to lawfully override the Division SOP Manual if doing so facilitates the safe and logical management of the situation.

 

7.3.            Any time the Division SOP Manual is overridden due to an unusual situation involving an increase in workload or activity level within the Communications Center, the employee acting in authority must:

 

7.3.1.      Establish contact with the Deputy Director for Emergency Communications and give a verbal report summarizing the situation.

 

7.3.2.      Complete a written report summarizing the situation, including steps taken by the employee before overriding the Division SOP Manual and justification for the override.  This will be submitted to the Deputy Director for Emergency Communications for review and additional comments and then be forwarded to the Director of Public Safety.

 

7.4.            Any time the Division SOP Manual is overridden due to an unusual situation involving an immediate threat of life and/or safety of Communications Center personnel, the employee acting in authority must:

 

7.4.1.      Establish contact with the Deputy Director for Emergency Communications or their current designee as soon as safety would not be compromised.  A brief verbal report will be given summarizing the situation.

 

7.4.2.      Complete a detailed written report, to be submitted to the Deputy Director for Emergency Communications, summarizing the situation, including steps taken by the employee before and after overriding the Division SOP Manual and justification for the override.

 

8.      Exchange of Information

 

8.1.            It is important to maintain a consistent flow of information throughout the chain of command. All employees should encourage and support the exchange of information with specialized and support components for the purpose of coordinating activities.

 

8.2.            It is imperative to maintain communication, coordination and cooperation among all agency employees. Information can be communicated through shift training, staff meetings, e-mail, voice mail and other bulletins or postings.

 

9.      Goals and Objectives

 

9.1.            It is the policy of the Emergency Communications Division to formulate and annually update written goals and objectives for the Division, and to translate them into measurable objectives. Each year the Deputy Directors will seek suggestions for Division goals from all Division personnel. With appropriate staff input and assistance, the Deputy Directors will review all suggestions and finalize a list of goals and objectives for the coming year. Selection of goals should be based on known priority orders and the increasingly broad scope and sequence of the communications mission. Goals and objectives shall be made available to all Division personnel on the intranet.

 

9.2.            Employee participation is vital in identifying goals and objectives which will aid in the accomplishment of the Division’s mission and enhance the efficiency and effectiveness of operations. All Division personnel are therefore encouraged at all times to submit proposals in writing, through the chain of command.

 

9.3.            The Deputy Directors, Assistant Directors, and the supervisory staff will meet as needed to review and evaluate the progress made towards attaining the set goals and objectives as well as review operational and training issues facing the Division.

 

10.  Mission Statement

 

“The Montgomery County Emergency Communications Division is a Public Safety Answering Point for police, fire, emergency medical and emergency management services within the County. Our mission is to: serve as the primary communications contact point between the citizen and the public safety agencies; accurately identify the citizen’s location and public safety response needs; quickly and efficiently activate the needed public safety services, and; provide all necessary communication support and coordination for all public safety and support agencies within the County.”

 

In carrying out our mission we recognize that service is our one and only product and our continuing goal is to provide it at a level superior to any other communications center. The source of our strength rests in our people who define and shape the organization through their individual and unique contributions developed in a spirit of cooperation and teamwork.

 

11.  Service Area

 

11.1.        The service area of the Emergency Communications Division includes all of Montgomery County, which consists of 38 townships and 24 boroughs. The size of the county is approximately 483 square miles in area; is close to a rectangle in shape, about 15 miles by 35 miles, with the longer side on a northwest/southeast axis; and is bordered to the southeast by Philadelphia, the northeast by Bucks County and a portion of Lehigh County, the northwest by Berks County, the southwest by the Schuylkill River (with Chester County on the other side) and by Delaware County.  The most dominant physical border is the Schuylkill River, which forms about two-thirds of the southwest side. Individual agency boundaries are maintained in the Computer Aided Dispatch (CAD) geographic database.       

 

11.2.        The communications center receives 9-1-1 and other emergency phone calls placed from locations within our jurisdictions. We also receive non-emergency phone calls. The center provides call processing and dispatch services for 91 Fire Departments, 22 EMS Squads, 50 Police agencies and other various County agencies.

 

12.  Concurrent Jurisdiction

 

Occurrences of concurrent jurisdiction are areas within Montgomery County where state police and municipal police officers can have jurisdiction to respond to calls. 

 

12.1.        Within Municipal Boundaries

 

12.1.1.  By practice, the municipal police officers will be dispatched as priority within their jurisdiction (e.g., Lower Frederick, Marlborough). The source of the concurrent jurisdiction is Pennsylvania Code Title 71, Part I, Chapter 2, Article VII, Section 250 and 252, giving the Pennsylvania State Police jurisdiction throughout the State of Pennsylvania.

 

12.1.2.  Back-up support between the municipal police and the state police may be provided when requested by the municipal police department.

 

12.2.        On State Highways

 

12.2.1.  Calls occurring on sections of state highways such as Route 309 and Route 422 that are not covered by municipal police are under the sole jurisdiction of the Pennsylvania State Police and CAD will generate an event for the state police only. Calls for these areas will be handled in accordance with SOP 6.1 Call Handling, Section 5 Transferred Calls.

 

12.2.2.  Calls occurring on sections that are covered exclusively by municipal police will only generate a call for that department; however, it is at the discretion of the municipal police to request back-up from the state police. If municipal police request back-up from the state police, the event shall be copied to the state police in CAD and the state police shall be notified.

 

12.2.3.  Calls occurring on sections that are concurrently covered by both the state police and municipal police will generate an event in CAD for both the state police and the municipal police. Notification of the state police shall be made.
